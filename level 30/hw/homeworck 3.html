Demon Slayer

Demon Slayer ( Japanese: 鬼滅の刃, Kimetsu no Yaiba ) is a manga series by Koyoharu Gotōge . It has been published in Japan since 2016 and has been adapted into an anime television series, also known as Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba , as well as in other media. Manga and anime can be classified into the genres of action, fantasy and drama. They tell of the fate of two siblings, the younger of the two has apparently become a man-eating demon and the older one tries to find a cure for this condition. The franchise was exceptionally successful both in Japan and internationally. The title was number 1 on the list of best-selling mangas in Japan in 2020, the television series achieved high ratings and the first film in the series, Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba – The Movie: Mugen Train , was the most successful film worldwide in 2020.

Contents

The boy Tanjirō Kamado grows up as the eldest son of a charcoal burner. In order to provide for his five younger siblings and his mother, he works particularly hard and even in the depths of winter goes from the forest to the city to sell coal. There, the hard-working boy is popular with many. But when he returns to his family's hut one day, they have all been victims of an attack. According to the tracks, they were the demons that the legends tell of and that eat people at night. Only Tanjirō's sister Nezuko is still warm, so he takes her with him on the way to the city. On the way, however, it turns out that she has also become a demon and thus a danger. They meet Giyū Tomioka, the pillar (柱 hashira , English 'pillar, pillar' , a "pillar" or hashira is an outstanding member of the corps of demon hunters) of water. He wants to kill Nezuko, but Tanjirō wants to protect his little sister. A fight ensues, in which Nezuko also protects her brother. Tomioka ultimately lets them both live and sends them to his master Sakonji Urokodaki. On the journey to Master Urokodaki, the siblings meet another demon against whom they must defend themselves, and against whom Nezuko once again protects her brother. She herself has to hide from sunlight during the day in order not to decay and not be recognized as a demon by humans. Once at Urokodaki's, Tanjirō begins his apprenticeship with him in order to become a demon hunter and one day be able to help his sister become human again. Finally, with the help of two spirits, he succeeds in completing his apprenticeship and passing the demon hunter test: surviving seven days and nights on Mount Fujikasane, which is populated by demons. In the end, only a few of the candidates survived, including the fearful Zenitsu Agatsuma and the confident Genya Shinazugawa. They are given a raven as a messenger, who accompanies them and tells them their tasks.

anime

Studio Ufotable produced both an anime television series and a feature film for 2019. The film Kimetsu no Yaiba: Kyōdai no Kizuna, which was released in theaters on March 29, 2019, was directed by Haruo Sotozaki and consists of the first five episodes of the series. The television series then started on April 6, 2019, and was created by the same director, who also wrote the screenplays together with Takashi Suhara and Toshiyuki Shirai . Akira Matsushima was responsible for the character design and Koji Eto and Masaru Yanaka were the art directors . After the first season with 26 episodes, the film Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba – The Movie: Mugen Train was released in Japanese theaters, which also made up the six episodes following the first season. It was followed by the Entertainment District Arc with a further eleven episodes and the Swordsmith Village Arc with another eleven episodes, so that the series currently comprises 55 episodes. The broadcast in Japan began on October 13, 2019 on the channels BS11 , Gunma TV , Tokyo MX and Tochigi TV . At the same time, the anime is being released internationally with subtitles in several languages ​​via various online platforms. This includes streaming with German subtitles via Wakanim . The online publication was taken over by Crunchyroll after the platform was taken over by Crunchyroll , where the German dubbed version was later released. From August 27, 2021, the series was shown on ProSieben Maxx .
