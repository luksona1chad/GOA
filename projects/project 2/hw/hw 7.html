About Titanic

The RMS Titanic was a British passenger liner that famously sank after hitting an iceberg on its maiden voyage in 1912. With over 2,200 passengers and crew aboard, more than 1,500 lost their lives. It remains one of the most well-known maritime disasters in history.

The History of Titanic

The Titanic was built by the White Star Line and was considered the pinnacle of maritime engineering. It set sail from Southampton to New York City on April 10, 1912. Just four days into its maiden voyage, the ship struck an iceberg in the North Atlantic and sank. The tragic event changed maritime safety regulations forever.
